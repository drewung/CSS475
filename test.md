## Purpose

You're a UWB Data Visualization student who knows Tableau dashboards, R analysis, and data storytelling. But when you open job listings for entry-level data analyst roles, recruiters often expect more than your required coursework. This document explains how to close that gap before graduation by outlining best practices that help you build the technical profile recruiters actually screen for.

## Learn Python and SQL Outside of Required Coursework

### Why Employers Expect More Than R and Tableau

Python and SQL appear in the majority of data analyst job descriptions [1]. While Tableau and R are valuable for visualization and statistical work, Python is the dominant language for data cleaning, automation, and pipelines [2]. SQL is the primary way analysts query databases at nearly every company. Knowing only the tools your curriculum covers puts you at a disadvantage the moment your resume lands next to someone who learned both.

Power BI has become the dominant dashboarding tool in corporate environments, with many companies choosing it over Tableau due to its lower cost and integration with Microsoft 365 [2].

### Course Suggestions

Take **[CSS 475 (Data Management and Queries)](https://www.washington.edu/students/crscatb/css.html)** if space exists in your schedule. The course introduces SQL in a structured environment and provides experience with relational databases.  

At the same time, build Python fluency through consistent weekly practice using platforms like Coursera or DataCamp.

## Earning Certifications That Signal to Recruiters

### Why Certifications Matter

Certifications show that you can use specific tools already adopted in industry. They can reduce uncertainty for recruiters by demonstrating applied technical competence, as they are evaluating many entry-level candidates without years of professional experience [2].

### Which Certifications to Pursue

Start with the **[Google Data Analytics Certificate](https://grow.google/certificates/data-analytics/)** which introduces SQL, spreadsheets, and visualization tools in a structured program. It provides a broad foundation and is widely recognized by employers.  

After building that foundation, pursue the **[Microsoft Power BI Data Analyst (PL-300)](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/credentials/certifications/data-analyst-associate/?practice-assessment-type=certificationcertification)**. Power BI is widely used in corporate environments and signals familiarity with enterprise reporting tools.

## Building Projects Outside of Class

### Why Personal Projects Matter

Coursework often stays private and uses clean datasets, which does not reflect real-world work. A public portfolio shows how you wrangle data, define problems, and communicate results [4]. Projects also give you concrete examples to discuss in interviews, allowing you to explain your reasoning and decisions instead of only listing tools.

### How to Build Projects That Stand Out

Choose one dataset from a domain you care about (sports, public health, local government data) and one dataset from **[Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/)** or **[data.gov](https://data.gov/)** that is well-documented. Document your process, Post your project on GitHub with a clear README that explains your question, your method, and what you found. A Tableau Public dashboard or a GitHub Pages report is something you can link directly in your resume and LinkedIn.

## Practice Technical Interviews with a Peer

### Why Solo Practice Falls Short

Practicing alone does not prepare you to explain your reasoning under pressure. Interviews require clear communication while solving problems in real time [3].

### How to Structure Peer Practice Sessions

Practice with a peer who is preparing for similar roles. Alternate between interviewer and candidate so you develop both problem-solving and evaluation skills. Record feedback immediately after each session. Keep a short log of what you answered well and where you stumbled, patterns in that log tell you where to focus.

## IPR Graduation Requirements

### Why You Should Start Early

IPR completion can take multiple terms due to approvals and documentation. Starting early gives you flexibility to resolve issues without delaying graduation and disrupting your job search.

### How to Reach Out

Check your Individual Preparedness Requirement (IPR) status early by contacting UWB Academic Advising or scheduling an appointment through the MyUWB portal. Advisors can confirm your current progress and identify any missing requirements. More information about IPR and hands-on learning opportunities is available through the [UWB IAS hands-on learning page](https://www.uwb.edu/ias/undergraduate/hands-on-learning).
